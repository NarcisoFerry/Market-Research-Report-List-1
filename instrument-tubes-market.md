<p><p>The instrument tubes market is primarily divided into seamless and welded types. Seamless tubes are crafted without any joints, offering superior strength and resistance to pressure, making them ideal for high-demand applications. In contrast, welded tubes are formed by fusing edges together, providing a cost-effective solution for lower-pressure environments. Each type has its unique advantages, with seamless tubes favored for durability in critical situations, while welded tubes are appreciated for their versatility and ease of production in various industrial applications.</p></p>
