<p><ul><li>Centrifugation</li><li>Membrane Separation</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The disposable apheresis market comprises two primary technologies: centrifugation and membrane separation. Centrifugation utilizes centrifugal force to separate blood components based on density, efficiently extracting specific cells or plasma. In contrast, membrane separation employs selective permeability through specialized filters to separate components based on size and charge. Both methods are widely used in therapeutic applications, such as plasma exchange and stem cell collection, offering advantages in efficiency, sterilization, and reducing cross-contamination risks, thereby driving their growing adoption in clinical settings.</p></p>
