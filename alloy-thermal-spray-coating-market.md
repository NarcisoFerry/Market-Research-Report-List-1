<p><p>The Alloy Thermal Spray Coating market comprises two primary types: the Combustion Flame Process and the Electrical Process. The Combustion Flame Process involves heating alloy powders using a flame to create a protective coating on surfaces, ideal for high-temperature applications. In contrast, the Electrical Process utilizes an electric arc to melt and spray the alloy material onto surfaces, offering precise control and better adhesion. Both methods enhance resistance to wear, corrosion, and thermal effects, serving various industrial needs.</p></p>
