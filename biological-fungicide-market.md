<p><p>The biological fungicide market is primarily categorized into two main types: microbial and botanical. Microbial fungicides utilize naturally occurring microorganisms, such as bacteria or fungi, to combat pathogens, effectively enhancing soil health and crop resilience. In contrast, botanical fungicides are derived from plant extracts and essential oils, offering a more sustainable approach with minimal environmental impact. Both types aim to reduce chemical pesticide reliance, promote eco-friendly agricultural practices, and improve crop productivity while ensuring safety for non-target organisms.</p></p>

<p><p>The biological fungicide market is segmented into four main applications: seed treatment, soil treatment, foliar spray, and chemigation. Chemigation combines fungicide application with irrigation, allowing for effective distribution throughout the root zone, optimizing disease control and promoting crop health.</p></p>
