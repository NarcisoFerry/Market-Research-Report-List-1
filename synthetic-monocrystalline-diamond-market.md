<p><p>The synthetic monocrystalline diamond market is primarily categorized into two production methods: High-Pressure High-Temperature (HPHT) and Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD). HPHT diamonds are created by mimicking the natural formation process under extreme pressure and temperature, resulting in diamonds with unique characteristics. Conversely, CVD involves vaporizing carbon-containing gases to produce diamonds layer by layer in a controlled environment, allowing for greater customization and purity. Both methods cater to various industrial applications, including electronics, optics, and cutting tools.</p></p>
