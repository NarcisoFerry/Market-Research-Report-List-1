<p><p>The lithium-ion battery market features two main types of foils: copper foil and aluminum foil. Copper foil is primarily used for the anode due to its excellent electrical conductivity, enhancing battery performance and energy efficiency. In contrast, aluminum foil serves as the cathode current collector, providing structural integrity and resistance to corrosion. Both foils are critical in optimizing battery efficiency, lifespan, and overall energy density, catering to the growing demand for electric vehicles and portable electronic devices.</p></p>
