<p><p>The organo-phosphorus market is categorized into three main types based on the coordination number of phosphorus compounds. Three-coordinated phosphorus compounds typically include phosphites and phosphonates, primarily used in agriculture and pharmaceuticals. Four-coordinated phosphorus compounds, such as phosphonamides and phosphoramides, find applications in agrochemicals and plastics. Five-coordinated phosphorus compounds, often used in specialty chemicals and advanced materials, exhibit unique reactivity. Each type serves distinct industrial needs, driving growth in sectors like pesticides, herbicides, and performance polymers.</p></p>
