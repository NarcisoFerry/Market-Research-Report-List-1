<p><p>The electric vehicle battery market can be categorized into several types based on chemistry and application. NCM (Nickel-Cobalt-Manganese) and NCA (Nickel-Cobalt-Aluminum) batteries offer high energy density, ideal for long-range vehicles. LFP (Lithium Iron Phosphate) provides safety and longevity but lower energy density. LCO (Lithium Cobalt Oxide) is known for efficiency in small devices, while LMO (Lithium Manganese Oxide) balances power with thermal stability. Other emerging technologies aim to enhance performance, sustainability, and cost-effectiveness in EVs.</p></p>
