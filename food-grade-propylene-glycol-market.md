<p><p>The food grade propylene glycol market comprises three main types: Mono Propylene Glycol (MPG), Dipropylene Glycol (DPG), and Tripropylene Glycol (TPG). MPG is widely used as a solvent and humectant in food products, enhancing flavor and moisture retention. DPG serves as an emulsifier and is often found in processed foods, while TPG, less common, is primarily used in specialty applications. Together, these glycols support various food formulations, ensuring quality and stability in food and beverage products.</p></p>
