<p><p>The Polyurethane Resin for Leather market encompasses two primary processing methods: dry and wet processes. The dry process involves applying resin to leather surfaces to create a durable, flexible finish without introducing moisture, enhancing resistance to wear and environmental factors. In contrast, the wet process incorporates resin during the leather tanning stage, saturating the material for a deeper, more integrated finish. Both methods cater to diverse applications in the leather industry, affecting texture, durability, and aesthetic qualities.</p></p>
