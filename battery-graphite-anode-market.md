<p><p>The battery graphite anode market is primarily divided into two types: natural graphite and synthetic graphite. Natural graphite is sourced from mines and is known for its cost-effectiveness and high capacity. It's often used in lower-cost battery applications. Synthetic graphite, produced from petroleum coke or other precursors, offers superior performance, including higher purity and better conductivity, making it ideal for high-performance batteries like those in electric vehicles. Both types serve distinct needs in the growing energy storage sector.</p></p>
