<p><p>External plug-in adapters are categorized into three main types based on their output characteristics: AC/AC adapters convert alternating current (AC) from a wall socket to a different AC voltage for devices like lamps. AC/DC adapters convert AC to direct current (DC), commonly used for charging devices like laptops and phones. DC/DC adapters regulate and convert one DC voltage level to another, typically utilized in automotive or portable electronics to power applications requiring specific voltage levels. Each type serves distinct device needs.</p></p>
