<p><p>The automotive LiDAR sensor market comprises two main types: mechanical LiDAR and solid-state LiDAR. Mechanical LiDAR utilizes rotating components to capture 3D environmental data, offering high-resolution imaging and wide range coverage but potentially increasing complexity and maintenance needs. Solid-state LiDAR, on the other hand, features a simpler design with no moving parts, enhancing durability and reliability while potentially reducing costs. Both types are crucial for advancing autonomous vehicle technologies, enabling safer navigation and obstacle detection in various driving conditions.</p></p>
