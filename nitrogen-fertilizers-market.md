<p><p>The nitrogen fertilizers market is primarily categorized into two types: ammonia and granular urea. Ammonia is a key nitrogen source, often used in its anhydrous form or as ammonium sulfate, promoting rapid plant growth and enhancing crop yields. Granular urea, on the other hand, is a widely used solid nitrogen fertilizer, known for its high nitrogen content and slow-release properties, making it effective for varying soil types. Both types play vital roles in modern agriculture, supporting sustainable food production.</p></p>
