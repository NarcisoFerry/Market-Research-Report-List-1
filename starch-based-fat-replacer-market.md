<p><p>The starch-based fat replacer market includes various types, primarily microparticulated starch and modified starch. Microparticulated starch is processed to create small particles that mimic the texture and mouthfeel of fats, making it suitable for low-fat food applications. Modified starch, on the other hand, is chemically or physically altered to enhance its functional properties, such as thickening and stability, allowing for improved texture in various food products. Both types serve as effective alternatives to traditional fats, catering to health-conscious consumers.</p></p>
