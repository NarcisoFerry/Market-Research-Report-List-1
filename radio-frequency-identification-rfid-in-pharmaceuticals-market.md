<p><p>Radio-Frequency Identification (RFID) in the pharmaceuticals market enhances supply chain efficiency and drug safety. Chipped RFID utilizes tags with embedded microchips for precise tracking of medications, enabling real-time inventory management, serialization, and counterfeit detection. In contrast, chipless RFID relies on passive tags that utilize printed antennas for identification without microprocessors, offering a cost-effective solution for tracking bulk items. Both types significantly improve traceability, streamline operations, and ensure compliance with regulatory standards in the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
