<p><p>The CRISPR gene editing tools market consists of several key components. Cas nucleases are enzymes that cut DNA at specific locations, enabling precise edits. Guide RNA directs the Cas nuclease to its target site, ensuring accuracy in gene editing. DNA ligases facilitate the joining of DNA strands post-editing, while restriction endonucleases serve to cut DNA at predetermined sequences for various applications. Other tools may include delivery mechanisms and additional editing techniques, enhancing the versatility of CRISPR technologies in research and therapeutics.</p></p>
