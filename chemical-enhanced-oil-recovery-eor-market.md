<p><p>Chemical Enhanced Oil Recovery (EOR) involves the injection of chemicals into oil reservoirs to improve oil extraction rates beyond what is achievable through primary and secondary recovery methods. This technique enhances the mobility of oil and reduces its viscosity, facilitating greater extraction efficiency. Key chemicals used in this process include surfactants, polymers, and alkalis, each playing a critical role in altering the fluid properties of the oil and reservoir rock.</p><p>The market for Chemical EOR is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing global energy demand and the need to maximize output from existing oil fields. With aging oil reserves becoming less productive, operators are turning to EOR techniques to sustain production levels. The Chemical Enhanced Oil Recovery Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.1% during the forecast period. </p><p>Latest trends in the market include advancements in chemical formulations and improved injection technologies, which are enhancing recovery efficiencies. <p><p>Chemical Enhanced Oil Recovery (EOR) involves the use of chemicals to improve oil extraction from reservoirs. The market is categorized into three types: Petro-based, Bio-based, and Water-based. </p><p>Petro-based EOR uses synthetic chemicals derived from petroleum, which enhance oil flow but may pose environmental risks. Bio-based EOR employs biodegradable and renewable materials, offering a more environmentally friendly alternative. Water-based EOR focuses on using water and surfactants to modify viscosity and mobilize trapped oil, emphasizing cost-effectiveness and efficiency in oil recovery.</p></p>
