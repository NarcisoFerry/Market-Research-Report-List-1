<p><strong>The Optical Grade Silicon Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Czochralski (CZ) Method</li><li>Float Zone (FZ) Method</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The optical grade silicon market comprises two primary methods of production: the Czochralski (CZ) method and the Float Zone (FZ) method. The CZ method involves melting silicon in a crucible and pulling a single crystal to form a cylindrical ingot, suitable for various optical applications due to its high purity and uniformity. In contrast, the FZ method uses a zoned heating approach, melting silicon in a controlled manner to achieve even higher purity, making it ideal for advanced optical applications. Both methods cater to distinct market needs, with FZ generally preferred for more demanding specifications.</p></p>
