<p><p>Wheat starch is categorized into two primary market types: industrial grade and food grade. Industrial grade wheat starch is utilized in non-food applications, such as adhesives, paper manufacturing, and textiles, due to its thickening and binding properties. In contrast, food grade wheat starch is specifically processed for human consumption, serving as a thickener, stabilizer, or texturizer in various food products like sauces, soups, and baked goods. Each type is tailored to meet the specific requirements and safety standards of its intended use.</p></p>
