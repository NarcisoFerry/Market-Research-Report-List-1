<p><p>Hydrogen sulfide scavengers are chemical agents used to remove hydrogen sulfide (H₂S) from gas and liquid streams, ensuring safety and environmental compliance. The market is divided into regenerative and non-regenerative types. Regenerative scavengers can be reused after H₂S absorption, allowing for cost efficiency and sustainability. In contrast, non-regenerative scavengers react with H₂S irreversibly and must be replaced once saturated. Each type is selected based on application requirements, efficiency, and operational costs in various industries, including oil and gas.</p></p>
