<p><p>The Embedded Flash (eFlash) market consists of three primary types based on storage architecture: Single Level Cell (SLC), Multi-Level Cell (MLC), and Triple Level Cell (TLC). SLC stores one bit per cell, offering high performance and endurance, making it ideal for critical applications. MLC stores two bits per cell, balancing cost and performance for consumer devices. TLC stores three bits per cell, maximizing storage density and cost-effectiveness, but with reduced speed and durability, suitable for general-purpose use.</p></p>
