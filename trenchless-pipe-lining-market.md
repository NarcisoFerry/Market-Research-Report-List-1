<p><p>The trenchless pipe lining market encompasses several advanced techniques for rehabilitating underground pipelines without extensive excavation. Trenchless sliplining involves inserting a new pipe into an existing one, while Cured-In-Place Pipe (CIPP) uses resin-impregnated liners that cure in place to form a new, seamless pipe. Spray-In-Place Pipe (SIPP) applies a sprayable resin that solidifies to restore pipe integrity. Pipe bursting replaces old pipes by breaking them apart while simultaneously installing a new one. Other methods may include various innovative trenchless technologies.</p></p>
